Cement Industry

What is cement:

Powdered materials have plastic flow while mixed with water
have property of settling
to hard solid structure with varying degree of strength and bonding properties

Portland cement: Small amount water
In few hours it would be hard like stone
Main raw materials: limestone, bauxite and clay
Portland cement is a calcium alumina silicate compound and is manufactured from limestone and clay (or shale) with minor amounts of iron oxide, silica sand and alumina as additives where required to balance the mineral composition.

Manufacture

1. Wet process
2. Dry process
1. Wet process: Europe and Asia Limestone and clay are soft, moist weather and cheap fuel
2. Dry process: USA Limestone and clay are hard and fuels are cheap The process selection was depends on: Physical condition of raw materials Climate Cost of fuel Dry process: A little low quality cement

Glass Industry

Physical properties of Glass

1. Non-crystalline, often transparent amorphous solid
2. Completely vitrified product
3. Hard and rigid : no definite melting point
4. High viscosity
5. Insulator of electricity

Manufacture of Glass

Formation of batch material
Melting
Shaping
Annealing
Finishing

Special Glasses

Fused silica glass

- made by fused pure silica but not transparent
- made by high temperature pyrolysis of silicon tetrachloride
- used for telescope mirrors
Properties : - low specific gravity (2.07)
- High softening point (1427 oC)
- low coefficient of thermal expansion
- Highest annealing point (1074 oC)
- Unusual chemical stability

High silica glass

- 96% SiO 2 and 3% B2O3 and alumina
- made from borosilicate glass heating: silica as separate layer HCl treatment at 98oC second heat treatment furnishes the glass
- used in laboratory wares: condensers, crucibles etc.
Properties :
- low specific gravity (but > 2.07)
- High softening point (1500 oC)
- low thermal conductivity
- High annealing point
- High chemical stability
- Transparent
- high temperature fluctuation resistance

Optical glass

- phosphorus and lead silicate with cerium oxide
- Cerium oxide: absorb ultra violet light
Properties :
- High specific composition
- low viscosity
- long annealing
- free from bubbles
- sustain optical clarity

Lead glass

- lead oxide + silica + alkali
- 92% lead
- used for optical work; electric bulbs, neon tubing etc.
Properties :
- High refractive index
- Low hardness
- High dispersion power
- high density

Ceramics Industry

What is ceramics?

An inorganic, non-metallic, often crystalline oxide, nitride or carbide material:carbon or silicon (clay), may be considered ceramics.
Ceramic materials are brittle, hard, strong in compression, and weak in shearing and tension

Use of ceramics

Construction and decoration
Manufacture of metals (refractory materials)
Manufacture of chemical products (stoneware and porcelain)
Sanitation
Insulation in electrical industry

Ceramics Industry: Bangladesh aspect

One of the exports oriented and fast growing sectors
Almost 80% of total demand is being met by local industries
Trade liberalization policy required:
reduction of tariff rates
simplification of tariff structure
minimization of non-tariff barrier
simplification of trade law

SWOT Analysis Export & Growth Potential of Ceramic Industries in Bangladesh

Bangladesh’s ceramic has competitive advantage having low cost man power and availability of gas, whereas interest for the capital and production capacity at an international standard is still weakened the industry. It has opportunity to grow further because of a substantial local and untapped international market for export. To some extent the industry faces unlawful competition for cheap import and also a great concern over health risk of ceramic tableware. Health risk can be minimized through improvement of product quality but price remains a threat to survive from import.
